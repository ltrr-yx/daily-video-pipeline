# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Developers, creators, and technically comfortable operators who clone the public repository and want to turn their own source lists into short vertical videos. They are choosing reusable templates, local configuration, and safe publishing defaults without exposing private sources, positions, strategy, or personal research focus.

## Product Purpose

Daily Video Pipeline is a privacy-first source-to-video toolkit. It helps users understand the available narrative templates, scene components, visual themes, privacy boundaries, and rendering commands so they can produce their own source-backed videos with BGM.

## Brand Personality

Clear, precise, and composed. The product should feel like a capable production desk: practical enough to run, polished enough to trust, and restrained enough to keep attention on source-backed information.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS hero pages, decorative dashboards, vague AI workflow visuals, oversized marketing copy, and design that suggests private investment advice or hidden proprietary source lists. The public experience should not look like a personal portfolio, broker terminal clone, or hype-driven social media template pack.

## Design Principles

- Show the operating model first: story choice, scene choice, visual theme, rendered video.
- Separate editorial structure from visual output so users understand what each layer controls.
- Use examples that are public, generic, and safe to fork.
- Make previews concrete enough to choose from, not merely decorative.
- Preserve trust through clear privacy language and source-review cues.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for documentation and gallery pages. Keep diagrams understandable without color alone, support reduced motion, and use responsive layouts that remain readable on small screens.
